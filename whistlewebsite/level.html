LEVELS OF DRYWALL FINISHING

USG WEBSITE

Level 1 Drywall

Whistle Con. uses this in all concealed spaces, such as above drops and behind plumbing walls also called "fire tape". Mud and tape all the joints

$0 there is no extra cost nor credit for doing this finish

Level 2 Drywall

Whistle Con. uses this on all mechanical rooms that do not have regular access. Tape and 1st coat nails and skims. (the area must be dried in and free of obstructions. if not level 1 will be applied)

$0 there is no extra cost nor credit for doing this finish

Level 3 Drywall

this is the finish we use on all living and public areas (this is a tape and 2 coats over skims, metal and nails. one over angles, and then we add our texture

$0 this is included in all our contracts, unless otherwise noted

Level 4 Drywall

This is used when A smooth finish is wanted. 3 coats on skims metal and nails 2 on angles texture on ceilings and sand

cost %10 of contract value. We will do the clubhouse smooth if asked at no extra cost, not to be confused with level 5 smooth. level 4 should not be painted with glossy, semi gloss, nor similar paint.

Level 5 Drywall

This is used when a smooth finish is wanted and glossy paint will be applied. 3 coats on skims metal and nails, 2 on angles, texture on ceilings or smooth and an extra coat evenly on %100 of the walls and ceilings sand and light check. guaranteed no defects detected by the untrained eye

$%50 of contract value. this can only be done by finishers with 6 plus years of experience and is really hands on

LEVEL 3 DRYWALL

Level 3 Typically used in areas which are to receive heavy texture (spray or hand applied)(we consider whistle texture to be a heavy texture since it covers 80% of the walls) finishes before final painting, or where com- mercial-grade (heavy duty) wall coverings are to be applied as the final decoration. This level of finish should not be used where smooth paint- ed surfaces or where lighter weight wall coverings are specified. The prepared surface shall be coated with a drywall primer prior to the application of final finishes. All joints and interior angles shall have tape embedded in joint com- pound and shall be immediately wiped with a joint knife or trowel, leav- ing a thin coating of joint compound over all joints and interior angles. One additional coat of joint compound shall be applied over all joints and interior angles. Fastener heads and accessories shall be covered with two separate coats of joint compound. All joint compounds shall be smooth and free of tool marks and ridges. The prepared surface Finishing Drywall Systems 157 5 158 shall be covered with a drywall primer prior to the application of the final decoration.
